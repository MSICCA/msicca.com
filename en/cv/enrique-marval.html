Enrique Marval

Chief of Operations (COO) | Lead Electrical Engineer · MSICCA

Mission Profile

Enrique Marval bridges the gap between US Federal Standards (NEC/NFPA/OSHA) and local operational realities in the Zulia region. With nearly two decades of field experience in hostile and austere environments, he specializes in critical infrastructure stabilization and leads MSICCA deployment teams to execute mandates with local logistical precision, full regulatory compliance, and strict safety protocols.

Core Competencies (Operational)

  • Regulatory bridge: translation of US execution requirements (NEC 2023 / IEEE Std 142) into local action plans, mitigating liability risks for Prime Contractors through strict QA/QC.
  • Facility hardening: rapid assessment and retrofitting of dilapidated infrastructure to restore operational capacity (power, HVAC, water).
  • Grid stability and power assurance: design and deployment of prime and backup power generation, synchronization, and fuel logistics integration.
  • Supply chain resilience: management of local procurement logistics, vetting suppliers to ensure a clean supply chain free from sanctioned entities (FCPA compliant).

Past Performance (Technical Complexity)

  • Critical water infrastructure stabilization: electrical system assessment and re-engineering for municipal water well fields and pumping stations, restoring supply capability under severe grid instability.
  • Healthcare facility retrofit (Category 1 spaces): electrical hardening of intensive care units and surgical theaters in public health infrastructure, delivering isolated power systems compliant with safety codes for life-support equipment.
  • Renewable energy integration (remote locations): conceptual and detailed engineering for photovoltaic systems (microgrids) in off-grid regions, providing autonomous power for humanitarian logistics hubs.
  • Mass transit power systems: QA/QC supervision for traction substations and passenger stations within a major urban rail system, validating compliance of electromechanical assemblies prior to commissioning.
  • International grid interconnection analysis: engineering review for a 7.91 MW photovoltaic generation system (USA), verifying interconnection compliance with National Grid (13.2 kV) standards.

Codes & Classifications

  • Role NAICS: 541330 (Engineering Services), 238210 (Electrical Contractors).
  • Standards adherence: NEC 2023, NFPA 70E, OSHA 1926.

Education & Vetting

  • B.S. Electrical Engineering – Universidad Rafael Urdaneta.
  • Vetting status: fully traceable professional history, no political exposure, cleared for work with international agencies.
